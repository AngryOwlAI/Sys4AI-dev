# TX-39 Remaining Evidence-Families Future-Work Disposition

## Decision

`DDR-SFADEV-STRATEGIC-BASELINE-G11-016` classifies each of the three remaining evidence families as retained active future work rather than a completion requirement for the current bounded non-production strategic-baseline migration:

1. Broad stakeholder and affected-party review.
2. Target-domain acceptance.
3. Production and operational evidence.

The decision supersedes only `G11-015`'s unresolved post-TX-38 route for these three dispositions. It preserves the independent-evaluation future-work disposition and every completed TX-38 and TX-37 artifact.

## Rationale

- Stakeholder and affected-party review requires identified external representatives, participation, concerns, dissent, conditions, and accountable disposition that repository evidence cannot supply.
- Target-domain acceptance requires a named target context, domain-specific evaluation, and accountable specialist acceptance; domain-neutral framework validation is not domain truth.
- Production and operational evidence requires a target runtime, authorized operator, environment, monitoring, incident and recovery exercises, maintenance ownership, production approval, and operational grant. Those conditions are outside this non-production migration baseline.

## Preserved Evidence and Limitations

- Each obligation remains active future work; none is deleted, waived, completed, or evidenced.
- No stakeholder or affected-party engagement was executed.
- No domain evaluation or acceptance was executed.
- No production runtime, monitoring, incident, maintenance, or operational work was executed.
- No production readiness, operational authority, stakeholder consensus, affected-party acceptance, domain truth, domain acceptance, permission expansion, or `G-10` acceptance is established.
- Canonical PRDs, requirement trace states, waivers, frozen TX-23 and TX-25 evidence, accepted TX-35 measurement, TX-37, TX-38, and prior activated history are unchanged.
